description: Active card priority is intentionally unformalized — organic daily rotation is a standing design decision

No formal priority ordering exists for active cards. Cards naturally assume more or less prominent roles on a daily basis based on external forcing functions (deadlines, energy, available time). This is intentional.

**Why:** Active cards span distinct contexts (coursework, job search, fitness, degree) that self-prioritize organically. A rigid priority convention would add overhead without changing day-to-day behavior. The user has sufficient external conventions in place to keep all active cards in mind.

**Key distinction:** Intentional deprioritization (a conscious decision, often captured as `Deferred` or a progress note) is different from passive drift (other cards temporarily taking precedence). The latter is tolerated and self-correcting — the user circles back naturally. `Deferred` is reserved for a genuine decision to pause, not for slow patches.

**How to apply:** Do not propose a card priority convention. The absence of one is a deliberate design decision, not an open question.
