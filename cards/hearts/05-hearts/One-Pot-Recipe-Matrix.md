### Recipe #1: Chickpea & Spinach Curry
**Servings:** 6-8 | **Protein:** 15g/serving | **Time:** 30 min

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cans (15oz each) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 1 can (14oz) coconut milk
- 1 can (14oz) diced tomatoes
- 1 large onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
- 2 tbsp curry powder
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1/2 tsp turmeric
- 4 cups fresh spinach
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 2 tbsp olive oil

**Method:**
1. Heat oil in large pot over medium heat
2. Sauté onion until softened (5 min)
3. Add garlic and ginger, cook 1 min
4. Add curry powder, cumin, turmeric; toast 1 min
5. Add tomatoes, coconut milk, chickpeas
6. Bring to simmer, cook 20 min
7. Stir in spinach until wilted (2 min)
8. Season with salt and pepper
9. Serve over rice or with naan

### Recipe #2: Black Bean & Sweet Potato Chili
**Servings:** 8-10 | **Protein:** 18g/serving | **Time:** 40 min

**Ingredients:**
- 3 cans (15oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
- 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed (1-inch)
- 1 can (28oz) diced tomatoes
- 1 large onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp chili powder
- 1 tbsp cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp cayenne (optional)
- 4 cups vegetable broth
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 2 tbsp olive oil

**Optional toppings:** Avocado, cilantro, lime wedges, tortilla chips

**Method:**
1. Heat oil in large pot over medium-high heat
2. Sauté onion until softened (5 min)
3. Add garlic, cook 1 min
4. Add spices, toast 1 min
5. Add sweet potatoes, tomatoes, black beans, broth
6. Bring to boil, reduce to simmer
7. Cook 25-30 min until sweet potatoes are tender
8. Mash some beans/potatoes for thickness (optional)
9. Season with salt and pepper
10. Serve with toppings

### Recipe #3: Lentil Bolognese Pasta
**Servings:** 6-8 | **Protein:** 20g/serving | **Time:** 35 min

**Ingredients:**
- 1.5 cups brown or green lentils, rinsed
- 1 lb pasta (penne, rigatoni, or spaghetti)
- 2 cans (14oz each) crushed tomatoes
- 1 large onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 carrots, finely diced
- 2 celery stalks, finely diced
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tbsp Italian seasoning
- 1 tsp oregano
- 4 cups vegetable broth
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Fresh basil for garnish

**Method:**
1. Heat oil in large pot over medium heat
2. Sauté onion, carrots, celery until softened (8 min)
3. Add garlic, cook 1 min
4. Add tomato paste, Italian seasoning, oregano; cook 2 min
5. Add lentils, crushed tomatoes, broth
6. Bring to boil, add pasta
7. Reduce to simmer, cook 15-20 min stirring occasionally
8. Add water if too thick
9. Season with salt and pepper
10. Garnish with fresh basil

### Recipe #4: White Bean & Kale Soup
**Servings:** 8-10 | **Protein:** 15g/serving | **Time:** 30 min

**Ingredients:**
- 3 cans (15oz each) white beans (cannellini or great northern), drained
- 1 large bunch kale, stems removed, chopped
- 3 carrots, diced

- 1 large onion, diced
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- 8 cups vegetable broth
- 2 tsp dried rosemary
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 2 bay leaves

**Method:**
1. Heat oil in large pot over medium heat

8. Remove bay leaves
9. Season with salt, pepper, lemon juice

### Recipe #5: Peanut Noodle Stir-Fry
**Servings:** 4-6 | **Protein:** 18g/serving | **Time:** 20 min

**Ingredients:**
- 12 oz rice noodles
- 2 cups shelled edamame (frozen is fine)
- 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell peppers, snap peas, carrots, broccoli)
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
- 1/3 cup peanut butter (natural, creamy)
- 3 tbsp soy sauce or tamari
- 2 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp maple syrup or agave
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1/4 cup water (to thin sauce)
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- Crushed peanuts, green onions, lime wedges for garnish

**Method:**
1. Cook rice noodles according to package, drain
2. In small bowl, whisk peanut butter, soy sauce, vinegar, maple syrup, sesame oil, water
3. Heat vegetable oil in large wok or pot over high heat
4. Add garlic and ginger, cook 30 seconds
5. Add vegetables and edamame, stir-fry 5 min
6. Add cooked noodles and peanut sauce
7. Toss everything together for 2-3 min
8. Garnish with peanuts, green onions, lime

**Note:** Best eaten fresh, doesn't freeze well

### Recipe #9: Thai Peanut Curry
**Servings:** 6 | **Protein:** 22g/serving | **Time:** 30 min

**Ingredients:**
- 1 block (14oz) extra-firm tofu, pressed and cubed
- 1 can (15oz) chickpeas, drained
- 1 can (14oz) coconut milk
- 3 tbsp peanut butter (natural)
- 2 tbsp soy sauce or tamari
- 1-2 tbsp red or yellow curry paste
- 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell peppers, broccoli, carrots)
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
- 1 lime (juice)
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- Fresh basil or cilantro for garnish

**Method:**
1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in large pot over medium-high heat
2. Add tofu, cook until golden on all sides (8 min), set aside
3. Add remaining oil, sauté garlic and ginger 1 min
4. Add curry paste, cook 1 min
5. Add coconut milk, peanut butter, soy sauce; whisk smooth
6. Add vegetables and chickpeas
7. Simmer 15 min until vegetables are tender
8. Return tofu to pot, add lime juice
9. Garnish with fresh herbs
10. Serve over rice or noodles

### Recipe #11: Smoky Black Bean Soup
**Servings:** 8-10 | **Protein:** 17g/serving | **Time:** 35 min

**Ingredients:**
- 3 cans (15oz each) black beans, drained (reserve liquid from 1 can)

### Recipe #14: Vegan Jambalaya
**Servings:** 6-8 | **Protein:** 15g/serving | **Time:** 35 min

**Ingredients:**
- 1.5 cups long-grain white rice
- 2 cans (15oz each) kidney beans, drained
- 1 can (14oz) diced tomatoes
- 1 green bell pepper, diced
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 1 onion, diced
- 3 celery stalks, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 cups vegetable broth
- 2 tsp Cajun seasoning
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp cayenne (adjust to taste)
- 1/2 tsp thyme
- 2 bay leaves
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Green onions and hot sauce for serving

**Method:**
1. Heat oil in large pot over medium-high heat
2. Sauté onion, bell peppers, celery until softened (8 min) - this is the "holy trinity"
3. Add garlic, cook 1 min
4. Add Cajun seasoning, paprika, cayenne, thyme; toast 1 min
5. Add rice, stir to coat (2 min)
6. Add tomatoes, beans, broth, bay leaves
7. Bring to boil, cover, reduce to simmer
8. Cook 20 min until rice is tender and liquid absorbed
9. Remove bay leaves
10. Fluff with fork, season with salt and pepper
11. Serve with green onions and hot sauce

### Recipe #15: Ethiopian Lentil Stew (Misir Wot)
**Servings:** 6-8 | **Protein:** 16g/serving | **Time:** 30 min

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cups red lentils, rinsed
- 1 large onion, finely diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
- 2-3 tbsp berbere spice mix (Ethiopian spice blend)
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 4 cups vegetable broth
- 1 can (14oz) diced tomatoes
- Salt to taste
- 3 tbsp olive oil or vegetable oil

**Berbere Spice (if making your own):**
- 2 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp cayenne
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1/2 tsp coriander
- 1/2 tsp fenugreek
- 1/4 tsp cinnamon
- 1/4 tsp cardamom
- 1/4 tsp black pepper

**Method:**
1. Heat oil in large pot over medium heat
2. Add onion, cook until very soft and starting to brown (10 min)
3. Add garlic, ginger, berbere spice; cook 2 min
4. Add tomato paste, cook 2 min
5. Add lentils, broth, tomatoes
6. Bring to boil, reduce to simmer
7. Cook 15-20 min until lentils are very soft
8. Stir frequently, add water if too thick
9. Season with salt
10. Serve with injera bread or over rice

### Recipe #16: Buddha Bowl Base (One-Pot)
**Servings:** 6 | **Protein:** 19g/serving | **Time:** 30 min

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
- 1 can (15oz) black beans, drained
- 1 can (15oz) chickpeas, drained
- 2 sweet potatoes, cubed
- 2 cups broccoli florets
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 3 cups vegetable broth
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 2 tbsp olive oil

**Tahini Dressing (mix separately):**
- 1/4 cup tahini
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 2 tbsp water
- 1 clove garlic, minced

10. Serve bowls drizzled with tahini dressing
11. Optional toppings: avocado, seeds, greens

### Recipe #17: Vegan Ramen Bowl
**Servings:** 4-6 | **Protein:** 20g/serving | **Time:** 25 min

**Ingredients:**
- 12 oz ramen noodles (or any Asian noodle)
- 1 block (14oz) extra-firm tofu, pressed and cubed
- 1 cup shelled edamame
- 4 cups vegetable broth
- 2 cups mushrooms, sliced
- 2 cups bok choy or spinach, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
- 3 tbsp soy sauce or tamari
- 1 tbsp miso paste
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- Green onions, nori, sesame seeds for garnish

**Method:**
1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in large pot over medium-high heat
2. Add tofu, cook until golden (8 min), set aside
3. Add remaining oil, sauté garlic, ginger, mushrooms (5 min)
4. Add broth, soy sauce, bring to boil
5. Add noodles, cook according to package (3-4 min)
6. Stir in miso paste (don't boil after adding)
7. Add edamame and bok choy, cook 2 min
8. Add sesame oil
9. Divide into bowls, top with tofu
10. Garnish with green onions, nori, sesame seeds

**Note:** Best eaten fresh, prep components separately for meal prep

### Recipe #19: Mushroom & Lentil Bourguignon
**Servings:** 6-8 | **Protein:** 15g/serving | **Time:** 45 min

**Ingredients:**
- 1.5 cups brown or green lentils, rinsed
- 1 lb mushrooms, quartered
- 3 carrots, chopped into chunks
- 2 celery stalks, chopped
- 1 large onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced

- 1 cup red wine (or additional broth)
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tsp thyme

- Fresh parsley for garnish

1. Heat oil in large pot over medium-high heat

4. Add garlic and tomato paste, cook 2 min
5. Add wine, scrape up browned bits
6. Add lentils, broth, soy sauce, bay leaves, thyme, rosemary
7. Bring to boil, reduce to simmer
8. Cook 25 min until lentils are tender
9. Return mushrooms to pot
10. Remove bay leaves
11. Season with salt and pepper
12. Serve over mashed potatoes or with crusty bread
13. Garnish with parsley

## Meal Prep Tips

### Batch Cooking
- Make 2x or 3x recipes on prep days
- Most recipes yield 6-8 servings
- 2 recipes = 12-16 meals for the week

### Storage
- **Refrigerator:** 4-5 days in airtight containers
- **Freezer:** 3 months in freezer-safe containers
- Label with: Recipe name, date, protein content

### Reheating
- **Microwave:** 3-4 min from fridge, 10-12 min from frozen
- **Stovetop:** Add splash of water/broth, heat gently
- **Soups/stews:** May need extra liquid after freezing

### Boosting Protein
- Serve with baked tofu (10g protein per 3oz)
- Serve with tempeh (15g protein per 3oz)
- Add protein powder to smoothies for breakfast
- Snack on nuts, seeds, or hummus between meals

## Notes for 2,900-3,000 Calorie Target

- Breakfast: 600-700 cal (smoothie, oatmeal, or tofu scramble)
- Lunch: 800-900 cal (one-pot meal + side)
- Dinner: 800-900 cal (one-pot meal + side)
- Snacks: 600-700 cal (fruit, nuts, protein bar, hummus & veggies)

**One-pot meal portion sizes:**
- Aim for 400-500 cal per serving of one-pot meal
- Add whole grain (rice, quinoa, bread) for additional 200-300 cal
- Side salad or roasted vegetables adds 100-150 cal
- Total meal: 700-950 cal

**Hitting protein targets (210-240g/day):**
- Each meal with one-pot recipe + side: 25-35g protein
- 3 meals × 30g = 90g
- Breakfast with protein focus: 30-40g (tofu scramble, protein smoothie, oatmeal with nuts/seeds)
- Snacks: 30-40g (protein bar, protein shake, hummus, nuts)
- **Total: 210-250g protein**
